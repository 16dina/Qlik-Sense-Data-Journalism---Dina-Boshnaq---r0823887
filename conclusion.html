Conclusion and Recommendations

Recommendations for policy makers and legislators to regulate the Airbnb market based on the data analysis

View Conclusion

We've analyzed a lot of data and gained insights into the Airbnb market in terms of the different Airbnb types, the dynamic of the hosts, the availabilities distribution, and the economic impact of Airbnbs. Armed with this data, policymakers can make informed decisions to strike a balance between the economic benefits of Airbnb and the potential drawbacks it poses to housing availability, community stability, and affordability. It's crucial to consider implementing regulations that address the following key points:

  • Assess and potentially regulate Airbnb pricing to prevent distortion of the traditional housing market and ensure affordability for residents.

    Evaluate the economic impact of short-term rentals on the local economy and consider measures to mitigate any negative consequences on housing costs and availability.

    Address the concentration of Airbnb listings among a small number of hosts, especially those owning multiple properties, to prevent market dominance and maintain a diverse housing landscape.

    Strengthen and enforce host verification processes to ensure the legitimacy of Airbnb listings and maintain trust within the community.

    Consider implementing restrictions on the year-round availability of entire homes as short-term rentals to preserve housing stock for long-term residents.

    Ensure that Airbnb hosts are paying their fair share of taxes, contributing to local infrastructure, public services, and community development.

    Consider earmarking a portion of Airbnb-related tax revenues for local community projects, affordable housing initiatives, and infrastructure improvements.
