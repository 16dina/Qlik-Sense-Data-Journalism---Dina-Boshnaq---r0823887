Year round or Seasonal?

In Antwerp, most Airbnbs are available year round or more than 3 months in total per year. In Brussels, the majority of Airbnbs are available occasionally (less than 2 months in total), but there's still many that are available year round or often. As for Ghent, the numbers are close with 301 available year-round, 396 often, and 317 occasionally. The number for the third category (seasonally 2-3 months) is the least in all 3 cities.

If we were to consider Airbnbs that are available year round (more than 9 months) or often (3-9 months) as properties for commercial use, then that means 5648 out of 9498 (~60%) of Airbnbs fall under this category of properties. This can cause disruption in housing and communities if not regulated properly.

And if we were to check the number of availabilities for each month, we find that August has the most being 149123 which is a huge number. If we do the math (149123/9498), then that's about 16 days in August where every single one of those properties in all 3 cities is available for rent as an Airbnb. And even with the least month in terms of availability, April, it has 83700 availabilities, which if we were to assume all Airbnbs are being rented out at the same time, then it's about 9 days with all listings available.

This is to give you a better idea of how heavily these properties are used as Airbnbs instead of housing for the residing population.
